# AI Studio — Builder's Toolkit / Delegation Plan

Maps the six AI Fluency capabilities to Automation / Augmentation / Agency, specifically for building AI Studio — not content creation in general. Each row has its own distinct justification; none reuse another row's reasoning.

| Capability | Mode | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| **Empathy** | *Outside the spectrum* | Not an AI-involvement question at all for this build. Understanding the persona's pain (the CapCut rabbit hole, the script-craft gap, the time scarcity) came from being the persona — lived experience, not something AI supplied discretion, judgment, or execution toward. Where AI *did* help early on (descoping, feasibility calls) was actually Design/Architecture work wearing an Empathy label — real, but misfiled. There's nothing here for AI to do; forcing it into Automation/Augmentation/Agency would be a category error. |
| **Design** | **Augmentation** | I define the flow and product intent (what B-roll means for this product, what the Editor/Writer split does); AI translates that into implementation implications — what a Remotion composition needs to contain, what's technically feasible, level of effort. Genuine two-way shaping: I contested and changed AI's proposals repeatedly (B-roll scope, chat mechanism, age/gender gating) and won real changes. This is the capability where I have the most standing to push back, because it's product judgment — only I know what serves the persona. |
| **Architecture** | **Augmentation** *(low-agency form)* | Big, mostly one-time structural decisions — LangChain vs. LangGraph, RAG, MCP scope. I can't genuinely contest these yet; the honest reason is a knowledge gap, not lack of interest. AI has to teach while deciding, and I review/accept based on growing understanding rather than independent judgment. Different from Design specifically because I don't yet have the standing to argue back — I'm along for the ride while learning, not steering from parity. |
| **Implementation** | **Augmentation** *(high-agency form)* | Ongoing, repeated micro-decisions *within* an already-accepted architecture — what exactly goes into a RAG query, whether age/gender enters a specific prompt, how a schema field is structured. Distinct from Architecture because I actively and successfully contest these (the age/gender fight, the feedback-nesting bug I caught, the script-vs-video_analyzer_output redundancy) — not "I can't push back yet," but "I'm applying real judgment within a foundation I've already accepted." Not passive review; genuine co-shaping with me driving several of the final decisions. |
| **Judgment** | **Augmentation** | Split cleanly by what's actually checkable. AI handles technical/mechanical eval — does output validate against schema, does duration land in tolerance, is a beat matched. I handle subjective, human-facing judgment — does the SFX actually fit, does the video feel right, is this worth shipping. Consistent with the earlier decision to skip automated visual-quality eval/retry entirely (Editor spec) because that rubric was judged too subjective to trust — the same principle drives this row: AI doesn't get discretion over judgment calls that don't have a defensible mechanical check. |
| **Shipping** | **Automation** | Once the pipeline (build → test → release) is actually defined, executing it — version bumps, deploys, routine bug fixes — needs no ongoing discretion. This is the one capability where the "known path, AI just executes it faster" description genuinely applies, and it's correctly the only Automation row: everything upstream of it still involves either a knowledge gap (Architecture) or active contestable judgment (Design, Implementation, Judgment) that Shipping itself doesn't carry. |

## Why This Mapping Holds Up

Five rows land on some form of Augmentation, and one sits outside the spectrum — only Shipping is pure Automation. That's not a failure to differentiate; each Augmentation row is Augmentation *for a different reason*:

- **Design** — because product judgment is mine to hold, not a knowledge gap
- **Architecture** — because it *is* a knowledge gap, not yet a judgment call I can make independently
- **Implementation** — because I've moved past the knowledge gap into active, contested co-shaping
- **Judgment** — because the split is mechanical-vs-subjective, not about who knows more

The distinction between Architecture and Implementation is the one worth remembering for an interview: both are Augmentation, but one is "I'm learning while AI decides," the other is "I'm deciding while AI assists" — same label, opposite power balance, and the age/gender relevance-gating fight is the concrete example that proves Implementation is real co-shaping, not passive acceptance.
